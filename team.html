Why Now
Four forces converging.

LLMs just became good enough

Reliable resume scoring across 22 career fields, specific line edits, and thread-based coaching. Not possible 18 months ago.

50% of entry-level roles face AI disruption

Application volumes up, AI-generated resumes everywhere, six in ten applications are junk. Students need a way to stand out.

Career centers are losing relevance

1 advisor for every 2,263 students. Budgets flat, staff stretched. Dilly is a career center that never closes. 60x cheaper.

The "career score" doesn't exist yet

Credit scores transformed lending. A universal career readiness score could transform hiring. First mover builds the data moat. Every audit, every outcome, every hire makes the model smarter.
