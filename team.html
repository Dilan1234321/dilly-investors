Why Now
Four forces converging.

LLMs just became good enough

Claude and GPT-4 can reliably score resumes against complex rubrics, generate specific line edits, and carry coaching conversations. This wasn't possible 18 months ago.

Students are desperate for signal

Post-COVID hiring is harder, application volumes are up, and AI-generated applications mean recruiters trust resumes less. Students need a way to stand out.

Career centers are losing relevance

Budgets flat, staff stretched. Students want instant, personalized, available-at-midnight help. Dilly is a career center that never closes.

The "career score" doesn't exist yet

Credit scores transformed lending. A universal career readiness score could transform hiring. First mover builds the data moat.
