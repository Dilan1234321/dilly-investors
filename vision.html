The Vision

Six products. Two businesses. One profile.

Dilly today is the consumer app: Student, Seeker, Holder. That's the wedge. The vision is a career operating system that fans out in two directions. Vertical expansions on the consumer side. A B2B platform on top. Every branch runs on the same profile, the same codebase, the same Haiku 4.5 economics.

Consumer Verticals

The wedge is undergrads. The aperture is every high-stakes career path.

Each vertical takes the core profile-first product and deepens it for a specific track where the stakes are highest and the incumbents are weakest. Same Haiku 4.5 backbone. Same fit narratives. Same retention primitives. Different prompts, different job sources, different cohort weights.

Verticals · D2C Subscription
Vertical 01 Next

Dilly Medical

The profile-first platform for pre-med and med-school applicants. MCAT readiness, AMCAS essay coaching, clinical-hour tracking, letter-of-rec intelligence, school-fit narratives, and "Am I Ready" verdicts across the specific rubrics residencies actually weigh.

→ Why: med-school admissions is the highest-anxiety, highest-willingness-to-pay career path in America. Same engine, specialized prompts.
Vertical 02 Next

Dilly Law

Same treatment for pre-law and JD applicants. LSAT pulse, personal-statement coach, 1L résumé help, firm/clerkship fit narratives, and a clear read on which schools reach, match, and safety given the full profile.

→ Why: law admissions is a second-highest-stakes track with similar demand elasticity. Natural adjacency once Medical proves the pattern.
Vertical 03 Next

Dilly International

Everything international students need in one profile-aware surface. Visa status intelligence, H-1B sponsor job filtering, OPT/CPT timeline tracking, CV-format variants for home-country norms, and a fit narrative that actually accounts for work authorization.

→ Why: 1M+ international students in the US, underserved by every existing tool, and the one cohort where "the right job posting" is a legal question, not just a fit question.
Vertical 04 Shipping Now

Dilly Skill Lab

A curated learning surface sorted by cohort. Every student sees the specific skills, courses, certifications, and hands-on projects that move them forward in their path. YouTube library live in v0.1. Next: structured mini-programs, integrated project submissions, and a credential layer that feeds the profile.

→ Why: the profile identifies gaps. Skill Lab is the only place in the app that closes them. Shipped April 2026.
The B2B Platform

The same user base, sold a second time.

Once the consumer product has .edu-verified students with living profiles, two second-layer businesses unlock. Both are opt-in on the student side. Students always control visibility. Recruiters and universities pay for access to a candidate pool that no one else can assemble.

B2B Platform · Institutional Revenue
Platform 01 B2B · Built

Dilly Recruiter

A recruiter-facing dashboard that surfaces only opted-in students across all 22 cohorts, every one of them .edu-verified as a real student. Skill-indexed search, anonymized until a match is confirmed, company-advice on what to highlight, and outreach that lands in an inbox the candidate actually checks.

The recruiter surface is already 2,075 lines of shipped code. What comes next is the go-to-market, the pricing (seat-based), and the verified-badge trust that makes this a category where no job board can compete.

→ Why: recruiters spend billions on unqualified leads. A verified, opted-in, skill-indexed student pool is a fundamentally new supply curve.
Platform 02 B2B · Later

Dilly University

A university-facing dashboard that shows every school exactly where their students stand. Career-readiness by major, gaps by cohort, early signals of who's struggling (if they integrate transcripts and grades), pipeline analytics, and a direct route from a student's Dilly profile into Dilly Medical or Dilly Law when the advisor flags the match.

Tutoring centers finally see who needs help before the drop date. Career centers finally have data. Admissions finally has a feedback loop from graduation outcomes. A lot of potential here, most of it unrealized in the market today.

→ Why: universities spend hundreds of thousands on career-center software that produces nothing. Dilly gives them the outcome dashboard they've been asking for since the '90s.

Every product on this roadmap reads the same profile. That's the moat, the economics, and the vision in one sentence.
