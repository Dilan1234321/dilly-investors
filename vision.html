The Vision

Ten products. Two businesses. One profile.

Dilly today is the consumer app: Student, Seeker, Holder. That's the wedge. The vision is a career operating system that fans out in two directions. Consumer verticals covering every high-stakes life track. A B2B platform sitting on top. Every branch runs on the same profile, the same codebase, the same Haiku 4.5 economics. Each one takes weeks to ship because the engine is already built.

Consumer Verticals

The wedge is undergrads. The aperture is every high-stakes career path.

Each vertical takes the core profile-first product and deepens it for a specific track where the stakes are highest and the incumbents are weakest. Same Haiku 4.5 backbone. Same fit narratives. Same retention primitives. Different prompts, different job sources, different cohort weights.

The B2B Platform

The same user base, sold a second time.

Once the consumer product has .edu-verified students with living profiles, two second-layer businesses unlock. Both are opt-in on the student side. Students always control visibility. Institutions pay for access to a candidate pool that no one else can assemble.

B2B Platform · Institutional Revenue
Platform · Q2 '27 B2B · Built

Dilly Recruiter

A recruiter-facing dashboard surfacing only opted-in students across all 22 cohorts, every one .edu-verified as a real student. Skill-indexed search, anonymized until match, company-advice on what to highlight, outreach that lands in an inbox the candidate actually checks.

The surface is already 2,075 lines of shipped code. What comes next is pricing (seat-based), go-to-market, and the verified-badge trust that makes this a category no job board can compete in.

→ Why: recruiters spend billions on unqualified leads. A verified, opted-in, skill-indexed student pool is a fundamentally new supply curve. TAM: $25K+ recruiter seats at $200–400/mo = $60M–$120M ARR by Y5.
Platform · Q4 '27 B2B · Later

Dilly University

A university-facing dashboard showing every school exactly where their students stand. Career-readiness by major, gaps by cohort, early signals of who's struggling (if they integrate transcripts and grades), pipeline analytics, and a direct route from a student's profile into Dilly Medical or Dilly Law when the advisor flags the match.

Tutoring centers finally see who needs help before the drop date. Career centers finally have data. Admissions finally has a feedback loop from graduation outcomes.

→ Why: universities spend $500K+ per year on career-center software that produces nothing measurable. Dilly gives them the outcome dashboard they've wanted since the '90s. TAM: 1,500 institutions × $30K–$80K ACV = $45M–$120M ARR.
